Common Technical Terms

These technical terms may affect the selection and user experience of headphones. Understanding these terms can help better understand the performance and functionality of the products.

Patented Technologies
  • ACAA™ 3.0 Coaxial Dynamic Driver: ACAA™ is a patented audio technology from a certain brand, and 3.0 Coaxial Dynamic Driver may be a version of this technology or a specific upgrade for it. Coaxial dynamic driver typically refers to the headphone’s driver unit, and ACAA™ may refer to a special driver unit design or technology developed by the brand.
  • Hybrid Noise Cancelling Technology: Hybrid noise cancelling technology refers to headphones that combine multiple noise cancelling technologies, such as active noise cancelling and passive noise cancelling. Active noise cancelling cancels out external noise by emitting anti-noise, while passive noise cancelling isolates noise through the headphone’s structure and materials. Hybrid noise cancelling technology combines these two methods to achieve better noise cancelling effects.
  • ANC: Active Noise Cancellation, a technology that cancels or reduces external noise interference by emitting sound waves opposite to environmental noise, providing a better listening experience.
  • DSEE Extreme: A sound processing technology used to enhance music quality by improving the clarity and dynamic range of low-quality audio.
  • LDAC High-Resolution Audio: LDAC is a Bluetooth audio coding technology developed by Sony, capable of high-resolution audio transmission for a higher quality music experience. LDAC can provide higher bitrates in Bluetooth transmission, resulting in better audio quality.
  • aptX: A Bluetooth audio coding technology developed by Qualcomm aimed at providing higher quality audio transmission and improving sound quality.
  • AAC: Advanced Audio Coding, an audio encoding format commonly used for iOS devices. Bluetooth headphones that support AAC encoding can provide better sound quality.
  • Bluetooth Versions: Bluetooth versions are typically represented by numbers such as Bluetooth 4.0, Bluetooth 5.0, etc. Different Bluetooth versions indicate different supported features and performance. Generally, newer versions support higher data transfer rates, more stable connections, lower power consumption, etc. You can find Bluetooth version information in the product specifications or descriptions.
  • LE Audio Technology: A new Bluetooth audio technology, where LE stands for Low Energy, designed to provide higher quality, more stable audio transmission while reducing energy consumption, thus extending device battery life.
  • QuietSmart 2.0: A hybrid active noise cancelling technology that combines multiple noise cancelling technologies to provide superior noise cancelling effects, which may include features like intelligent environmental perception, multi-channel noise cancelling, and adaptive noise cancelling.
Special Features
  • Transparent Mode: A feature found in some active noise cancelling headphones that allows external sounds to pass through the headphones, enabling users to hear ambient sounds when needed.
  • IPX Rating: Used to describe the waterproof rating of headphones, such as IPX4, IPX5, etc., representing the headphones’ resistance to water, ranging from waterproof to sweat-resistant levels.
  • Hi-Res Audio: High-Resolution Audio, an audio standard defined by the Japan Audio Association, requiring audio sampling rates of at least 96 kHz/24-bit or higher to achieve higher quality music experience.
  • Closed-Back Headphones: Headphones that seal around the ears, providing better sound isolation.
  • Dynamic Headphones: Headphones that use dynamic drivers (transducers), which are a common type of headphone driver. The driver is the component responsible for producing sound, usually a small speaker. Here, the diameter of the driver is referred to as 5 millimeters.
  • Active Noise Cancellation (ANC): A technology built into headphones or earbuds that can collect environmental noise and generate sound waves opposite to it to eliminate or reduce the impact of external noise on hearing. Passive noise cancellation refers to the ability of headphones or earbuds themselves to block out external noise to some extent without the need for electronic devices or processors to generate opposing sound waves. Passive noise cancellation is usually achieved through well-sealed ear cups or ear tips.
  • Balanced Armature: Balanced armature drivers use one or more balanced arms with small magnets and coils attached to them. When current flows through the coil, it generates force in the magnetic field, causing the balanced arm to vibrate and produce sound. Balanced armature drivers are typically smaller than dynamic drivers and can provide higher frequency detail and accuracy. Balanced armature drivers are generally more suitable for high-frequency detail and mid-high frequency performance.
  • Dynamic Driver: Dynamic drivers are one of the most common driver technologies, consisting of a magnet and a diaphragm (also known as a coil) connected to it. When current flows through the coil, it generates a magnetic field that causes the diaphragm to vibrate, producing sound. Dynamic drivers typically have better low-frequency response and dynamic range. Dynamic drivers are more suitable for bass performance.

Buying Guide

With so many proprietary terms, it can be overwhelming. Here are concise purchasing pointers for different user groups:

  • Bass Lovers: Look for headphones with “Bass Boost” or “Deep Bass Technology” for richer bass effects. Consider headphones with “Dynamic Drivers” or “Closed-Back Design,” which typically deliver stronger bass performance.

  • Sports Enthusiasts: Choose headphones with “Sweatproof and Waterproof Features” to ensure they remain undamaged during workouts. Prioritize headphones with “Lightweight Design” and “Ear Stability Features” for stability and comfort during exercise.

  • Audiophiles with High-Quality Audio Demands: Look for headphones with “High-Resolution Audio Certification” or “Professional Music Brand Certification” for a high-quality music experience. Consider headphones with “Active Noise Cancelling Technology” or “High-Resolution Audio Technology” to provide purer, clearer sound quality.

  • Long-Term Wearers: Look for headphones with “Comfortable Ear Cushions” and a “Lightweight Design” to alleviate discomfort during long wear. Consider headphones with “Low-Pressure Ear Cushions” and “Ventilation Hole Design” for better breathability and comfort.

  • Wireless Headphone Users: Look for headphones with “Bluetooth 5.0 or Higher Versions” to ensure stable connections and lower power consumption. Consider wireless headphones with “Long Battery Life” and “Fast Charging Function” for convenience during extended use.

  • Users Needing Headphones for Meetings: Choose headphones with noise-cancelling microphones or clear voice capture features to provide a clear, interference-free voice call experience. Ensure headphones have stable wireless connectivity to avoid call interruptions or unstable signals. Choose headphones with convenient and easy-to-use control buttons or touch panels for volume adjustment, call control, and other operations during meetings.
